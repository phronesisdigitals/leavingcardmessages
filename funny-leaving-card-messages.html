Funny Leaving Card Messages That Actually Land

There's a big difference between a message that makes someone smile and one that gets an actual laugh. Here's how to write the latter — with real examples.

📖 4 min readUpdated 2025

Most "funny" leaving card messages aren't funny. They're safe. They're inoffensive. They're the written equivalent of a firm handshake. Nobody reads "off on a new adventure — how exciting!" and actually laughs.

Good humour in a card is specific, slightly self-aware, and punches gently. Here's how to get there.

The rules of funny leaving card messages

Rule 1: Specific is funny. Generic is not. "We'll miss you" is forgettable. "We'll miss you most at the 4pm Friday meeting where you were the only one keeping us all sane" is a laugh.

The best funny card messages have three things in common: they reference something real, they're slightly self-deprecating or situational, and they end warmly. You're ribbing someone you like, not roasting a stranger.

Funny messages for a close colleague

Example

"I'm not sad you're leaving, I'm sad that I now have to do both our jobs. Best of luck — you'll need it slightly less than I will."

Example

"You were the only person here who made Monday mornings bearable. This is genuinely a catastrophe. Congratulations on your escape."

Example

"Finally! I've been waiting for you to leave so I can apply for your job. (I'm joking. Mostly.) Go be brilliant."

Funny messages for a manager leaving

Trickier territory. The key is warm and affectionate rather than sharp. A leaving card isn't a roast.

Example

"We've set a very high bar for whoever replaces you. Specifically, the bar is 'doesn't make us want to cry in the stairwell.' You cleared it every day."

Example

"Thank you for pretending our ideas were good right up until they weren't. It's a rare skill. Good luck out there."

Funny messages for someone retiring

Example

"You're swapping Monday morning meetings for actual Monday mornings. We're not jealous. (We're extremely jealous.)"

Example

"Thirty years in and you're finally free. If you start to miss the commute, please seek help immediately."

Funny messages for someone going travelling

Example

"Off to see the world while we're stuck here debating the new printer policy. We hate you. Have the best time."

Example

"Please send photos of somewhere warm. We'll be looking at them from the exact same desk in the exact same office. Genuinely jealous. Go have fun."

What to avoid

  • Jokes that only work if you know the person very well — remember others will read the card too
  • Anything that could be read as bitter or passive aggressive — even if it isn't meant that way
  • Humour that's really just a complaint dressed up in an exclamation mark
  • Inside jokes that reference something HR might take issue with
  • Ending on a purely funny note — even funny messages land better with a warm sign-off
