Birthday Card Messages for Colleagues: What to Write

Professional but personal — how to write a birthday message for a colleague that feels genuine rather than copied from a generic list.

📖 4 min readUpdated 2025

Writing in a colleague's birthday card sits in a specific kind of awkward middle ground. You want to be warm, but not overly familiar. Funny, but professionally appropriate. Personal, but not presumptuous.

The good news is that the bar is low — most people write "Happy Birthday! Hope you have a great day!" and leave it at that. Anything with a bit more thought will stand out.

For a colleague you know well

This is where you have the most room to play. A shared memory, a reference to something you both find funny, or a genuine compliment all work well.

Example — warm

"Happy birthday! Working with you is genuinely one of the good things about coming in. Hope your day is as brilliant as you are."

Example — funny

"Another year older and somehow still the most energetic person in every meeting. Happy birthday — you're an inspiration and a mystery."

For a colleague you don't know very well

Keep it warm and genuine without pretending a closeness you don't have. A simple, sincere message is always better than a forced one.

Example

"Happy birthday! Hope you're able to celebrate properly — you deserve a great day."

Example

"Wishing you a really lovely birthday and a day that's entirely about you for once!"

For your manager or boss

Warm but professional. A genuine compliment works well here — just make sure it reads as sincere rather than strategic.

Example

"Happy birthday! Thank you for everything you do for the team. Hope you get a proper chance to celebrate today."

Example — slightly lighter

"Happy birthday! We promise to only need you for urgent things today. (We're lying, but we mean well.)"

For milestone birthdays

40th birthday

Example

"Welcome to your 40s — officially the decade where you stop caring what people think and start actually enjoying things. Happy birthday!"

50th birthday

Example

"50 looks brilliant on you. Here's to a decade that's every bit as good as the last one — and then some. Happy birthday!"

60th birthday

Example

"60 and still setting the standard for the rest of us. Happy birthday — hope it's celebrated in the best possible way."

Quick tip: Always add the person's name to the start of your message if you can. "Happy birthday Sarah!" immediately feels more personal than the same message without it — even if everything else is identical.
