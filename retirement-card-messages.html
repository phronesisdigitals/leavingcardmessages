Retirement Card Messages: What to Write for a Colleague

Retirement is a big milestone that deserves more than a generic message. Here's how to write something that genuinely honours someone's career.

📖 4 min readUpdated 2025

A retirement card is different from any other leaving card. It's not just about what's next — it's about what was. Someone is closing a significant chapter of their life, often after decades of work, and the card is one of the few tangible things they'll take with them.

It deserves more thought than "enjoy your free time!" Here's how to get it right.

What makes a good retirement message

Acknowledge the achievement. Years of service — whether it's 5 or 35 — represent something real. A good retirement message recognises that before looking forward to what comes next.

The best retirement messages balance three things: gratitude for what the person contributed, genuine warmth for who they are, and excitement for what's ahead of them. You don't need all three — even one done well is enough.

Warm and heartfelt messages

Example — long service

"After so many years of giving so much to this place, you more than deserve everything that comes next. It's been a privilege to work alongside you. Enjoy every single moment of this new chapter."

Example — close colleague

"Working with you has been one of the genuine highlights of my career. You've made this place better — and you've made me better too. Go and enjoy your freedom. You've earned it many times over."

Example — for your manager

"Thank you for everything you've done — for the team, for this place, and for me personally. You leave big shoes. Wishing you the most brilliant retirement."

Funny retirement messages

Example

"No more alarm clocks, no more meetings, no more replying to emails that could have been a text. We're genuinely jealous. Congratulations — go enjoy the life we all dream about."

Example

"You're leaving us to deal with all of this on our own. Honestly a bit selfish. Have a wonderful retirement — you've absolutely earned it."

Example — for a long-serving colleague

"Thirty years of turning up. Thirty years of putting up with all of us. If anyone deserves to sleep in on a Monday morning, it's you. Congratulations."

Short retirement messages

Sometimes simple is best — especially if the card is already full of messages.

Example

"You've given so much. Now it's time to enjoy every bit of what comes next. Congratulations and thank you."

Example

"What a career. What a person. Wishing you the most wonderful retirement."

If you barely know the person

A retirement card for someone from another department you've barely met still deserves something genuine. Keep it brief and warm.

Example

"Congratulations on your retirement — a brilliant milestone that deserves to be properly celebrated. Wishing you all the best in everything ahead."
